RFM Scoring and K-Means Clustering Implementation

1. RFM Scoring

RFM (Recency, Frequency, Monetary) scoring is a technique used to segment customers based on their purchasing behavior. Recency measures how recently a customer made a purchase, Frequency measures how often they purchase, and Monetary measures how much they spend. Microsoft Fabric's integration with Python and Power BI allows for easy calculation and visualization of RFM scores.

2. K-Means Clustering

K-Means clustering is an unsupervised machine learning algorithm used to group customers into distinct segments based on their behavior. In Microsoft Fabric, you can use Jupyter Notebooks to implement K-Means clustering and analyze customer segments. This helps in identifying high-value customers, at-risk customers, and other behavioral patterns.

3. Customer Segmentation

Customer segmentation involves dividing customers into groups based on their behavior, demographics, or other characteristics. Using RFM scores and K-Means clustering, you can create actionable customer segments. Microsoft Fabric's Power BI integration allows for interactive dashboards to visualize and analyze these segments.
